# Amazon Sales Analysis – Business Case Study

## 📌 Project Overview

This project explores Amazon product sales data to understand how pricing, discounts, and product characteristics influence customer engagement and purchasing behavior.

The goal is to move beyond basic data exploration and translate raw transactional data into actionable business insights, while also structuring the data for real-world analytical use through SQL.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Identify which product segments drive the most customer engagement
* Understand how pricing and discount strategies affect demand
* Analyze category-level performance and customer satisfaction
* Detect potential risks such as heavily discounted but low-rated products
* Design a structured SQL schema to support reporting and analytics workflows

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

* Customer activity is heavily concentrated in **budget and mid-range products**, making them the primary drivers of engagement.
* Discounts are most impactful in price-sensitive categories where customers respond strongly to promotions.
* Premium products receive fewer reviews but maintain **consistently high ratings**, indicating trust and perceived value.
* Electronics and everyday utility items generate the highest interaction and repeat demand.
* Excessive discounting on low-rated products may indicate quality or positioning issues that require review.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

* Focus marketing and inventory planning on high-volume affordable segments.
* Use discounts strategically to drive demand without eroding brand value.
* Strengthen best-performing categories that consistently attract engagement.
* Position premium products around quality rather than aggressive price reductions.
* Continuously monitor customer feedback to improve underperforming products.
